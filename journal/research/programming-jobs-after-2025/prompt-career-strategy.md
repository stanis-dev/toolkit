## What has already been researched

- **Post-2025 market baseline:** Software-development openings remained materially below pre-pandemic baselines through early 2026 in major advanced markets, even where the rate of decline stabilized.
- **Seniority polarization:** Senior/staff and manager-level roles held up better than junior and standard-level roles; junior hiring appears to be the most pressured segment.
- **AI inside mainstream roles:** Employers are absorbing AI expectations into ordinary software, systems, platform, and data jobs rather than mainly creating a separate "AI jobs" labor market.
- **Work rebundling:** The strongest interpretation is not simple job replacement, but cheaper code generation paired with greater importance for requirements, system design, debugging, validation, testing, security, and architectural ownership.
- **Likely stronger lanes:** Senior/staff IC, backend/platform, SRE/infrastructure, and systems-heavy roles look relatively better positioned than junior and narrower implementation-heavy roles.
- **Profile alignment:** My own profile is closer to the complementarity side of the market: senior/staff IC, backend/platform, AI-systems, performance optimization, infrastructure, and direct technical ownership.
- **Main unresolved questions:** How much of the market compression is specifically AI-driven versus macro/post-boom correction, whether junior hiring eventually recovers, whether cheaper software creation expands total demand enough to offset shrinking implementation-heavy work, and which specialized roles remain most durable.
- **Source quality notes:** The current research base is strongest on labor-market postings, company disclosures, and a handful of concrete org-design anecdotes. It is weaker on contractor-versus-full-time splits, QA/SDET demand, Europe role-level detail, and precise causal attribution.
