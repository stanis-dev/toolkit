# Communication Coaching Eval Set: Sierra Slack Supplement

Focused supplement for authored messages in the Sierra Slack workspace.

Purpose:

- evaluate communication in the arena where the target style is hardest
- apply the same quiet-confidence rubric with extra attention to **collapse-pattern risk**
- avoid over-correcting into coldness or false toughness
- serve as source evidence for hardening the **default** coaching standard, not for creating a separate Sierra-only mode

## Why this matters for the default standard

Sierra communication appears to combine:

- London-office communication norms
- startup pressure and brevity
- a more direct "what is the action / state / risk" expectation

That makes Sierra a poor place to derive the default from warmer or more verbose contexts. At the same time, not every softener or warm phrase is a problem. Some warmth may increase trust and collaboration. The goal here is not to punish friendliness. The goal is to identify when friendliness, self-deprecation, hedging, or over-accommodation **weakens clarity, authority, or response usefulness**.

## Additional review lens: collapse risk

For each item, assess collapse risk separately from the main rubric:

- **Low:** warmth/softening is either neutral or strategically useful
- **Medium:** some collapse-like signals, but likely not materially harmful
- **High:** the message meaningfully weakens authority, clarity, or decision pressure

Signals worth watching:

- apologizing where a simple update would do
- over-explaining your own state instead of the work state
- asking too softly when you need action
- self-undermining jokes immediately after good work
- excessive hedging in places where you have enough evidence to be direct

Do not treat these as automatic faults. Always ask:

- did this actually increase friction?
- did it reduce trust?
- did it weaken clarity?
- or did it simply add a small amount of warmth at low cost?

## Authority mechanism taxonomy

Each item is also tagged with its **primary authority mechanism**:

- `Clarity anchor`

- `Ownership signal`
- `Decision framing`
- `Formulation`
- `Calibrated pushback`
- `Strategic visibility`
- `Trust-preserving warmth`

## Immediate observations

- The Sierra-specific weak patterns are narrower than your self-perception suggests.
- There are real collapse-pattern signals, but they cluster around:
  - apology-first openers
  - self-undermining jokes immediately after competent work
  - over-softening a request when directness would be safer
  - meta-explanations about your own communication style

- There are also multiple cases where warmth is probably an advantage, not a flaw.
- The practical rule should therefore not be "remove friendliness."
- The practical rule should be:
  - remove friendliness that lowers clarity, priority, or authority
  - keep friendliness that preserves trust at low cost

## What to emphasize in the next instruction block

1. In Sierra-facing messages, default to action/state/risk first.
2. Do not apologize unless repair is actually needed.
3. Avoid self-undermining commentary after good work.
4. Ask directly when you need something; do not pre-negotiate the importance of your own ask downward.
5. Warmth is allowed, but self-positioning is usually not helpful.
