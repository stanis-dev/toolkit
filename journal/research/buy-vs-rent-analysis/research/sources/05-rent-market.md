## Scope, data caveats, and what ÔÇťrent inflationÔÇŁ means in Spain  
Your core modelling question (ÔÇťcan I assume stable rent for 30 years?ÔÇŁ) depends on **two distinct rent dynamics** that are often conflated:

**Sitting-tenant rent inflation (in-contract updates).** This is the change in rent paid by an existing tenant whose contract continues and whose rent is updated (if the contract allows it) under Spanish rental law (LAU). Under LAU, *rent updates are not automatic unless the contract explicitly provides for them*. During the term, rent can only be updated annually and ÔÇťin the terms agreed by the partiesÔÇŁ; if there is no explicit agreement, ÔÇťno rent update appliesÔÇŁ. ţłÇciteţłéturn62view2ţłü

**Market rent resets (between contracts).** When a contract ends (or a tenant is forced to move) and a new contract is signed, the rent is typically re-set to whatever the market will bearÔÇö**unless** special rules apply (e.g., ÔÇťstressed residential market areasÔÇŁ under the 2023 Housing Law, or other temporary caps that apply in certain times/contexts). SpainÔÇÖs legal protections strongly affect *how long you can stay*, but do not ÔÇťfreezeÔÇŁ rent for decades; the main long-run risk comes from **periodic involuntary moves**, after which rent can jump.

All *market price* statistics in this report (idealista, etc.) are based on **advertised (ÔÇťaskingÔÇŁ) rents**, not necessarily final signed rents. That can overstate (or sometimes understate) the rent actually paid, but it is often the best high-frequency local indicator available.

## Spanish rental law (LAU) and tenant protections  
### What counts as a protected ÔÇťmain residenceÔÇŁ contract  
LAU defines an **ÔÇťarrendamiento de viviendaÔÇŁ** as a lease whose primary purpose is to meet the tenantÔÇÖs **permanent housing need**. ţłÇciteţłéturn61view0ţłü  
It separately defines ÔÇťuse other than housingÔÇŁ and explicitly includes **seasonal leases** (ÔÇťpor temporadaÔÇŁ) as use other than housing. ţłÇciteţłéturn61view0ţłü  

This distinction matters hugely on the Castell├│n coast: a ÔÇťSeptember to JuneÔÇŁ or ÔÇťsummer seasonÔÇŁ contract can be common in tourist areas, but it typically **does not** provide the same long-duration protections as a true main-residence lease.

### How long can a tenant stay under LAU?  
Spanish tenancy stability is primarily delivered via **mandatory extensions** and **notice rules**.

**Mandatory minimum / ordinary stability logic:**  
The contract renewal rule in Article 10 states that once the contract reaches its initial expiry (or expiry of any renewals), **after at least five years** (or **seven years if the landlord is a legal entity**), if neither party has given notice of non-renewal within the required notice windows, the contract extends by **annual periods up to a maximum of three additional years**. ţłÇciteţłéturn64view4ţłü  

**Notice windows under Article 10:**  
- Landlord must notify **at least four months** before the relevant expiry date.  
- Tenant must notify **at least two months** before the relevant expiry date. ţłÇciteţłéturn64view4ţłü  

This structure implies a ÔÇťtypicalÔÇŁ long-term tenant who complies with the contract and pays rent can often remain **5ÔÇô7 years + up to 3 more** (i.e., potentially **8ÔÇô10 years**) *without having to renegotiate*, unless specific landlord grounds apply. ţłÇciteţłéturn64view4ţłü  

### Can rent increase during a contract?  
Under LAU Article 18:

- Rent can be updated **only once per year** (on the anniversary) and **on the terms agreed by the parties**. ţłÇciteţłéturn62view2ţłü  
- **If there is no explicit update clause**, *no update applies*. ţłÇciteţłéturn62view2ţłü  
- LAU also now references an **INE-defined ÔÇťreference indexÔÇŁ** intended to limit disproportionate increases: the law states the INE must define, before 31 Dec 2024, such an index ÔÇťas a reference limitÔÇŁ for Article 18 rent updates. ţłÇciteţłéturn62view2ţłü  

In practice: a well-negotiated contract clause (and the statutory framework around it) can make *in-contract* rent behaviour closer to ÔÇťindex-likeÔÇŁ rather than ÔÇťmarket-likeÔÇŁ.

## Key risks for a long-term renter in Spain  
### Risk of ÔÇťbecoming a seasonal tenantÔÇŁ in coastal Castell├│n
