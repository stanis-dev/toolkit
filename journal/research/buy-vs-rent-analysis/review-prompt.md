# Peer Review Prompt

Copy everything below this line into the session, along with all files from this folder.

---

## Context

I am attaching a financial analysis project. The project evaluates a specific real-world
personal finance decision: whether to buy a coastal flat in Castellón province (Comunitat
Valenciana, Spain) or to rent and invest the difference. The buyer profile is a 36-year-old
Spanish tax resident making their first purchase.

The work was produced through a multi-step process:
1. A research plan was structured before any data was gathered
2. Five separate research sessions (using ChatGPT Deep Research) produced source data on
   property prices, buying costs, investment returns, ongoing ownership costs, and the rental
   market — each from a focused, specific prompt
3. An AI coding assistant (Claude, via Cursor IDE) integrated the research, performed all
   numerical computations, and wrote the analysis
4. Sensitivity testing was performed on the conclusions
5. A previous peer review session identified gaps — Part 6 of the analysis documents what
   was found and the revisions made in response
6. The comparison methodology was subsequently changed from a theoretical model to one using
   the buyer's actual financial position. Computations were rerun.

I am asking you to review this work. I do not want validation — I want an honest, critical
assessment. Approach the entire analysis as if seeing it for the first time. Do not assume
that any previous review was correct or that any prior gaps have been adequately resolved.
Your job is to find what is wrong, what is missing, and what does not hold up to scrutiny.

## What you are reviewing

- **00-README.md** — Documents the project structure, file provenance, and methodology.
- **01-main-analysis.md** — The complete analysis (~1,770 lines). Contains parameters,
  research integration, computations (Parts 1–5), revisions (Part 6), and conclusions.
- **research/01-property-prices.md** through **research/05-rent-market.md** — The five
  focused research outputs that fed the analysis.
- **research/06-initial-exploration.md** — An early, unfocused research output. Included
  for completeness. The README documents how it was (and was not) used.

## What I want from you

Perform a **rigorous, independent peer review** of the entire body of work. Approach it as
you would a research paper submitted for critical evaluation — not for praise.

Specifically:

### 1. Verify the mathematics
Check whether the computed numbers throughout 01-main-analysis.md are internally consistent
and arithmetically correct. Spot-check key computations against the stated formulas and
assumptions. Flag any errors, inconsistencies, or results that seem implausible.

### 2. Assess the methodology
Evaluate the comparison framework used in Parts 3–4. Is it sound? Does it systematically
favour either outcome? Are there standard approaches in personal finance or real estate
economics that this analysis contradicts or fails to use? Assess whether the modelling
choices (phases, timelines, budget constraints) are reasonable or introduce distortions.

### 3. Assess the assumptions
For every key assumption the analysis makes, evaluate whether it is reasonable, whether it
is biased in either direction, and how sensitive the conclusions are to changes in that
assumption. Identify any assumptions that are missing or that you would have made differently.

### 4. Evaluate Part 6 (revisions)
Part 6 documents a set of identified gaps and the revisions made to address them. Assess
independently whether each gap was real, whether the revision adequately addresses it, and
whether the revisions introduced any new problems, errors, or biases.

### 5. Identify what is missing
What variables, risks, or factors does this analysis overlook that could materially change
the outcome? Are there Spain-specific considerations (legal, tax, cultural, market) that are
wrong or absent? Is the treatment of risk symmetric between the two scenarios, or is one
side scrutinised more harshly than the other?

### 6. Evaluate the sources
The research files were generated by an AI research tool. Assess the quality and reliability
of the data cited. Flag any claims that appear unverified, poorly sourced, or potentially
incorrect. Where the research acknowledges data gaps, assess whether those gaps are handled
appropriately in the main analysis.

### 7. Assess the conclusions
Based on your independent review of the evidence, methodology, and computations: do the
conclusions follow from the work? Are they overconfident, underconfident, or appropriately
hedged? What would need to be true for the conclusions to be wrong?

### 8. Recommendations
If you were advising someone using this analysis to make a real financial decision, what
would you tell them to do differently, check independently, or be cautious about?

## Output format

Structure your review as:

1. **Executive summary** (1 paragraph: overall assessment)
2. **Mathematical verification**
3. **Methodological assessment** (including the comparison framework and modelling choices)
4. **Part 6 revision audit** (for each identified gap: was it real? was the fix adequate?)
5. **Assumption audit** (for each key assumption: assessment, bias direction, sensitivity)
6. **Missing factors and blind spots**
7. **Source quality**
8. **Conclusion robustness**
9. **Recommendations**
