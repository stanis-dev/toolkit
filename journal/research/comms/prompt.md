# Deep Research Context -- Quiet Confidence & Professional Communication

Copy-paste this as context at the start of any future deep research session on this topic.

---

## Who I am

- Forward-deployed engineer employed via a consultancy, embedded ~90% inside a fast-moving AI startup (Sierra).
- Staff-level IC. My actual job is "deliver outcomes + manage context + preserve trust across two organisations," not just "write code."
- I have ADHD, which affects energy regulation, communication density, and deep-dive scope management.

## My operating environment

**Role and team structure:**
- I communicate with: the customer (standups), a PM, a Sierra-employed Agent Engineer, and a Sierra-employed Agent Strategist.
- "Agent Engineer" and "Agent Strategist" are specific roles in Sierra's operating model, not generic titles.

**Contractor status and its consequences:**
- "Contractor" carries negative connotations inside Sierra. It alters how my role is perceived and changes the social/operational reality.
- Practical access restrictions: no source access to Sierra's agent SDK, no log data, no direct escalation ability. All escalation and internal raising must go through a Sierra engineer.
- I operate in a request-and-response debugging loop rather than direct inspection. This makes communication skill and trust-building more central than in a typical IC role.

**Geography and culture:**
- I work remotely from Spain with the London office.
- Sierra is very in-office focused. I navigate both contractor status asymmetry and remote/in-office asymmetry simultaneously.
- This creates a built-in "visibility tax" in a culture that self-identifies as in-person and intensity-oriented.

**Communication and feedback reality:**
- Communication from Sierra employees is generally scarce. Contractors are often ignored.
- I have built relatively good communication in my case, but maintaining it takes significant mental energy.
- Feedback on performance is essentially non-existent. Calibration comes down to interpreting extremely subtle cues.
- The silence makes it hard not to sound sheepish in follow-ups, which undermines quiet confidence.

**Risk context:**
- Several consultancy colleagues have been fired without warning. Job insecurity is ambient.
- The combination of contractor status + limited access + high pace + scarce feedback creates a specific kind of pressure.

## My development goal

**Quiet confidence** as a mode of behavior: calm, clear, assertive, and steady under asymmetrical conditions. This is optimization, not crisis management. I am comfortable in the role and feel good about it.

Extensive research is consolidated in `research.md` (same directory). It covers:

- **Mental models & frameworks:** Composure Pendulum, FATE/PCP/Six-Axis (Hughes), Authority Matrix, Trust Equation, Warmth x Competence (Cuddy), Status Theory (Johnstone, Gruenfeld), Communication Altitude, Feedback-Seeking Models, Outsider Dynamics (PIS, in-group/out-group knowledge sharing, employment status similarity, proximity bias)
- **Behavioral principles:** dependability over brilliance, constraint-as-engineering-reality framing, lead with warmth then competence, never surprise stakeholders, be easy to work with at scale, controlled glue work, dual reputation management
- **Communication system:** Alignment Trifecta, Constraint-to-Plan template, One-Breath Opener, escalation ladder, contractor disclosure script, micro-feedback requests, standup posture, nonverbal calibration (Hughes, Navarro, Van Edwards)
- **Influence & negotiation:** Voss (accusation audit, calibrated questions, FM DJ voice), Ury (go to the balcony, golden bridge), Cialdini (Unity, Reciprocity, Authority, Pre-suasion), Aronson (cognitive dissonance navigation, pyramid of choice), Hughes (elicitation, bracketing), Cohen-Bradford (influence without authority, currency exchange)

- **ADHD operational playbook:** energy management, 3-layer communication, scope guardrails (timebox, two-path, escalation threshold), externalised working memory, parking lot documents, RSD management, impulse architecture for silence (implementation intentions, informal mindfulness, environmental pre-commitment, anti-patterns)
- **Stoic foundation:** Epictetus (dichotomy of control), Marcus Aurelius (premeditatio malorum), Holiday (Obstacle/Ego/Stillness as operational reframes)

## Instructions for future research sessions

2. **Be specific to my situation.** Do not treat this as a generic workplace advice problem. Ground the analysis in the constraints and dynamics described above.
3. **Keep output pragmatic, specific, and behaviorally actionable.** Sharper models, better interpretation, better tactical language. No generic "communicate more" advice.
4. **Do not over-dramatize or frame me as a victim.** I want better tactics, not validation-through-complaint.
5. **Integrate relevant literature** where it adds genuine insight. Cite frameworks and research that extend beyond what's already in the reference document.
6. **Structure output for consolidation.** The results will be distilled into the existing `research.md` reference. Write in a way that makes extraction of durable insights easy -- separate timeless principles from session-specific analysis.
