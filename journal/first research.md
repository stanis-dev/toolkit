## Why ÔÇťrent moneyÔÇŁ and ÔÇťmortgage moneyÔÇŁ are not the same maths

When you pay **rent**, essentially **100% of the payment is a consumption expense**: you get housing for that month, but you donÔÇÖt build an asset.

When you pay a **mortgage instalment**, it is split into two very different parts:

- **Interest** (a pure expense, economically ÔÇťrent you pay to the bank for the moneyÔÇŁ).
- **Principal** (a forced savings component that **reduces your loan balance** and increases your equity in the home).

- **Upfront transaction costs**, especially in Spain: *ITP/IVA*, notary/registry/gestor├şa, etc. (These costs are not recoverable and matter hugely in a 5-year horizon.)
- **Ongoing ownership costs** (IBI, community fees, maintenance, insurance).
- **Selling costs** (agency fees and other disposal frictions).
- **Opportunity cost**: the money tied up in down payment and costs could have been invested elsewhere.
